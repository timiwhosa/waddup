Cut Off Mark

      Every year, each department in Futa comes up with different cut off marks. Below, I will be explaining how this is derieved, calculated, factors considered and the cut off marks for all departments in Futa based on past records.

      For other details about Futa and futa Post-Utme, check out our other blog post below

      How is Futa cut off mark derieved?

      Firstly, what do we mean by "Cut off mark"? well, this is the minimum score a student must attain in other to be considered for admission into a particular institution.
      Now, How is it derieved?
      Cut off marks are calculated using "percentile". ie you know how many students you need, then you calculated to set a value (cut off) to cut the rest off. let me explain using the example below

      Example:
      Lets say a particular department need just twenty-five (25) students this year. But 156 students applied, hence,
      Total needed = 25;
      Total applicant = 156
      Applicant score sheet (Post-Utme and jamb percantage. note: how to derieve this is explained below);
      Number of students Percentage scored (%)
      50 40-50
      12 50-60
      60 60-70
      20 70-80
      14 80-90
      Total : 156
      Remember, the department only needs 25 students. What should they do. Well if i should guess, i'll say that all 14 that scored 85 will surely get admitted, then we will need at most 11 out of 20 from the 70% guys, right. This is where percentile comes in. We decide what percentage to set that will make sure we have just 25 students
      Percentile = lower class interval of percentile class +([((Percentile class x number of students)/100) - cf before percentile class]/frequency)x class size
      Number of students who score less than you = Number of students we don't need, ie Total applicant - Total needed = 156-25 = 131
      Hence, our percentile position is at: 131/156 x 100 = 83.97
      Reconstructing the table, we have:
      Number of students Percentage scored (%) CF
      50 40-50 50
      12 50-60 62
      60 60-70 122
      20 70-80 142
      14 80-90 156
      Total : 156
      percentile/ cut off = 60 +((83.97 x 156 / 100)-62/60 ) x 10 = 71.4988% aproximately 72%. Hence the minimum the department will Accept for jamb and post utme is 72%.
      If we repeat this example but with 50 students as the needed number, you will notice that the cut off drops to about 67.33%, which means a lower cut off.

      Moving on, We now know how cut off points are derieved, What are the factors you noticed that it(cut off depends on)? Well,

      • Number of applicants,
      • Number of needed applicants
      • Score of each applicant
      In summary, courses/ departments with high cut off marks are due to the fact that they have a lot of applicants, need just few and a lot of applicants have high %. Note that because a department has high cut off, doesn't make it more important.

      How can you calculate your cut off

      Very simple, just
      Divide your jamb score by 8,
      Divide your Post-Utme score by 2 and add both together
      ie 250 in jamb and 70% in post utme = 250/ 8 + 70/ 3 = 66.25
      You can do the reverse to predict the minimum you must have in your post utme or use this calculator
